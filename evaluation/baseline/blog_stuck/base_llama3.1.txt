What the hell is going on in there? You're staring at that blank page, feeling like a brick wall has been built around your creative juices. I get it – we've all been there.

You take a deep breath, trying to shake off the anxiety that's building up inside you. But nothing seems to work. Not even a good cup of coffee can crack open that dam of inspiration.

So here's what I do when I'm stuck: I force myself to show up, even if it feels like showing up is all I'm doing. That means taking my laptop out for a walk (yes, really – sometimes I write in the park), or doing some yoga on the living room floor while trying not to think about the looming deadline.

And then there's the tea. Or wine. Okay, let's be real – it's usually wine. But whatever your poison, take a sip and remind yourself that you're not failing. You're just paused.

Now, I know what you're thinking: "But Jacq, I've tried everything! I've changed my routine, I've meditated, I've rewritten the same paragraph 10 times." Yeah, me too. So we try something new. We take a break from our manuscript and do some freewriting – just letting the words flow without any intention of creating art.

It's like cleaning out your closet: you don't have to find the perfect outfit, you just need to start sorting through the mess. And sometimes, in the midst of all that chaos, something unexpected will pop up on the page. A character will emerge, or a plot twist will reveal itself.

The key is to trust the process – and yourself. Don't try to force it; instead, let your curiosity guide you back into the story. Ask yourself questions like "What if?" or "Who would this character say next?"

And when all else fails? Well, sometimes you just have to admit defeat – and that's okay too. It means taking a step back, re-evaluating what you're trying to achieve, and coming up with a new plan.

So here's your assignment for the day: show up, take a sip of whatever you need, and remind yourself that stuck is not failing. What will you create when you finally break free?
