Subject: The Beauty of Blemishes: Why Handwriting Still Matters

Hey writers!

Or should I say, hey pens? Because when was the last time you actually wrote something by hand?

I know what you're thinking: "Jacq, we're living in a world where our fingers are glued to keyboards and screens. Who needs handwriting anymore?" But here's the thing: with all the digital noise out there, I believe that handwriting is more relevant than ever.

Think about it - when was the last time you wrote a love letter, a gratitude journal entry, or a handwritten note to a friend? When did you last slow down enough to put pen to paper and let your thoughts flow onto a page?

Handwriting isn't just about communication; it's about connection. It's about showing up with intention, with effort, with a willingness to make mistakes (because, let's be real, our handwriting is never perfect).

So don't get me wrong - I'm not suggesting you ditch your laptop and go back to using a quill and inkwell. But what if we made space for both? What if we honored the imperfections of our handwriting as much as we do the precision of our typing?

I'll tell you why: because handwriting is like wine - it only gets better with age (and a few scribbles). It's an act of self-care, a way to slow down and savor the present moment. And when we take the time to put pen to paper, we're not just writing words; we're creating space for ourselves to breathe.

So go ahead, grab a pen, and let your thoughts spill onto the page (even if they're messy - especially if they're messy). Trust me: it's worth it.

What are you going to write with your next pen stroke?

Cheers,
Jacq
