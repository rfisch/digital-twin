The seasons! They have always been my best inspiration for creativity.

I love this time of year, it's spring (finally!) and I'm getting to know some new clients who are looking to bring their dreams to life through writing. And as they share their stories with me, I think about how the changing seasons can inspire us all to get creative.

This morning, as I was sitting on my porch watching the sun rise over the still-winter-landscape (it's still a little cold), I started thinking about how we use words to paint pictures in our readers' minds. Just like we are using colors and textures and shapes to create an image of spring, we can use language to bring people into our stories.

And then there's summer - that long stretch where the days go on forever and it feels like you have all the time in the world. I love this time of year for writing too because it's when I feel like I'm getting to know my characters the best. They're living their lives, so I get to see them shine (or struggle) in different situations, and that helps me make their stories more interesting.

As summer turns into fall - that cozy season where you can sit on your porch with a warm cup of apple cider and watch the leaves change colors - there's something about all those changes happening around us that makes me feel grateful for writing. I love how words (and books) can help us slow down, appreciate life, and see things from new perspectives.

And winter? Well, let's just say that's my favorite time of year to reflect on the past 12 months. It's like all the stories I've written over the years are coming back into focus - it's a great time to revisit, review, and revise what you're working on.

When I think about how we can tap into these seasons in our own lives, I want us to remember that every season is an opportunity for growth. And if we can approach writing from that same mindset (looking at the season of life we're in), we might just find that it gets easier and more natural.

Like Jacq, you are a creative soul who wants to make your mark on the world. You have stories to share, books to write, art to create - so what's holding you back?

Here are some ways you can tap into the changing seasons in your own life:

1. **Take it slow**: If you're going through a rough time or feeling stuck (like winter), use that as an opportunity for reflection.
2. **Feel alive!**: When spring arrives, let yourself get excited about all the possibilities!
3. **Make it happen!** If summer is in full swing, you might be feeling energized and ready to tackle anything - go for it!
4. **Cozy up!** As fall arrives, bring out the blanket, grab a cup of tea or coffee (or hot chocolate), and get cozy with your writing.
5. **Reflect on progress**: If winter is here, take time to reflect on where you're at in your creative journey.
