Letting Go of Perfectionism in Writing - The Bitter Truth (You're Not Perfect Either)

One day, my son asked me to read him his latest story. He was so excited and couldn't wait for me to see it. I told him that he's a great writer and to keep writing.

He looked up at me with those big brown eyes and said, "But mom, you're going to tell me something's wrong with it."

I knew then that my perfectionism is not just about me - it's contagious.

When I was younger, I had the same fear. Every time I'd write something, I thought it sucked. It wasn't good enough. I would rewrite and rewrite until I couldn't stand to look at it anymore.

It doesn't matter how old you are or what stage of your writing career you're in - perfectionism is a sneaky devil that can creep up on anyone.

And if we let it, it will suck the life out of us.

Perfectionism makes us feel like we need to have all the answers. We think we're supposed to know how to write without mistakes and that every book we read is better than ours. The more we read, the more we compare ourselves to others - which is a recipe for disaster if you want to be happy with your writing.

Here's the thing: perfectionism isn't just about our writing - it's also about what we think about our work. We often feel like people will judge us harshly and that they'll see all of our mistakes.

But here's the truth: no one is perfect, including you! And if you're comparing yourself to others, you're not even doing a good job at being human.

You need to let go of perfectionism in your writing. You can't do it because you'll always find something to fix - there will always be something that could be better.

And the thing is... it's okay!

Your readers want you to share your story with them, not a perfect one. They're willing to look past some minor mistakes (or even a few larger ones) if they connect with what you have to say.

You need to write like yourself and let go of perfectionism. It will make you happier as a writer - you'll be able to share your stories without fear, and that's where the magic happens!

Perfection is not something we can achieve or maintain in our writing (or any other part of life for that matter). The goal isn't to have perfect work. The goal should be to grow and learn from each piece you write.

And if all this is still too hard, ask yourself this question: "What's the worst that could happen?"

You might get some feedback or even some criticism. You might hear something that'll make you feel uncomfortable. But that's where the magic happens!

The moment you realize there's nothing you can do about it (and no one wants to judge you harshly) is when you start to let go of perfectionism.

And then, your writing becomes free.

Free from fear and free from comparison.

It's time to let go of perfectionism in your writing. Write like yourself, be authentic, and share your stories with the world!

Your readers will thank you for it!
