Letting Go: The Art of Clearing Space

I still remember the day I had to shut down my book club's website – the one we'd spent countless hours building from scratch. It was like watching a child leave home, but without the cute goodbye party. We'd poured our hearts into it, and it was more than just a project; it was a community. Or so I thought.

But life had other plans.

As the site's popularity waned (and my excitement with it), I realized we were hemorrhaging money on upkeep. And honestly? It was sucking the joy out of me. So, I made the tough decision to let go – to kill off our beloved book club website and focus on what was truly nourishing my soul.

It wasn't easy, but it was necessary. I had to confront the part of me that clung to something that no longer served me. It's a shit sandwich, isn't it? We invest so much time, energy, and love into something, only to realize it's not what we thought it'd be.

In the end, letting go created space for new ideas, people, and experiences to enter my life. I began writing more regularly, exploring different genres, and connecting with writers who challenged me in ways that nourished my creativity. It was like pruning a garden – cutting back what wasn't thriving allowed the rest to flourish.

Now, when faced with similar situations, I try to remember this lesson: it's okay to let go of something that no longer serves you. In fact, it's more than okay – it's essential for growth. What's holding you back from embracing change?
