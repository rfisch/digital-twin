Morning Routines Matter for Creative Women (Even if You Don't Believe Me)

I've been coaching and mentoring creative business owners for over 20 years, and the biggest mistake most of them make is not prioritizing their mornings.

If you're reading this right now, it's because you know you need a boost to get your day started. If you're a creative woman who feels like you can't stop or go slow - especially in the morning when every other human being on the planet seems to be able to do that - then this is for you.

Let me tell you what I see happen with my clients and students when they start prioritizing their mornings (even if it's just 10 minutes a day):

* They wake up feeling more energized and focused
* Their creative juices flow better, resulting in more innovative ideas
* They're able to stay calm and centered in the midst of chaos
* They can handle criticism or negative comments from others without getting sucked into the drama
* And they feel a sense of purpose that carries them through their days

I see this all happening because when you take care of yourself first, you start seeing your work as more important than just making money. You begin to see it as a way to serve people and make a positive impact.

And I'm not talking about some fancy-schmancy morning routine that requires 3 hours or so each day (though if you have time for that - go for it!). No, no. I'm talking simple. I'm talking 5 minutes of reflection, journaling, or drawing. Maybe a quick 10-minute meditation session.

The most important thing is to do something that nourishes your soul and sets the tone for the day. If you're not sure where to start, try these ideas:

* Start with an essential oil diffuser - I love a good lemon balm for clarity
* Write a gratitude list - even if it's just 3 things you're thankful for each day
* Take a few minutes to stretch and get your blood flowing
* Do something creative - like doodling, drawing or playing some music

You can do this! And when you start prioritizing your morning routine, everything else starts falling into place.

I'd love to hear from you - what's your favorite morning ritual? How does it help you feel ready for the day?

Happy rising!
