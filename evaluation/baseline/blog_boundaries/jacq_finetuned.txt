How to Protect Your Creative Energy (and Your Sanity)

I'm sure you've heard the phrase "creatives are sensitive souls." And it's true, we feel things deeply and can get burned out if we're not careful. But what happens when you push through that exhaustion and keep going?

You might think I'd tell you to just keep writing (or painting or dancing), but the truth is that pushing past your boundaries can lead to burnout. And once you burn out, it's hard to reignite the spark.

In my early days of being an entrepreneur (and artist), I would take on everything and everyone who asked for help. I'd work 24/7 on projects, then wonder why I was so exhausted all the time. When people asked me to do something last-minute, I would agree without hesitation, thinking that saying no would be unkind or selfish.

But I learned that when you say yes to others, you're actually saying no to yourself. And when you put your energy into everyone else's work instead of your own, you lose touch with what truly nourishes your creative soul.

Here are some tips for setting boundaries and protecting your energy:

1. Don't be afraid to say no (or "not now")

You'll never regret saying no to something that doesn't feel right for you. Trust me - I've been there many times, and it's always worked out just fine. You're not obligated to take on anyone else's work or project.

2. Set realistic expectations with your clients

If you're worried about burning out from doing too much for a client, talk to them about their expectations up front! It's okay to tell them that you have limited time and energy to devote to their project. You can say something like: "I'd love to work with you on this - but I only have 10 hours a week available. If you're happy with my availability, we can move forward."

3. Prioritize your own projects

If you're not working on your own stuff, how are you going to nourish and grow as a creative? You need to protect time for your own work - it's like giving yourself air.

4. Surround yourself with people who support you (and boundaries)

Being around negative people or those who don't respect your boundaries can be draining. If someone is pushing you to do more than you're comfortable with, remember that their needs are not your responsibility. You have the right to protect your time and energy - and if they don't understand that, it's okay to distance yourself.

5. Practice self-care

Burnout is usually a sign of ignoring or neglecting your own needs. If you feel drained all the time, ask yourself what you need to replenish your creative energy. Take care of yourself (physically, mentally, and emotionally) so that you can be at your best for others.

When I first started learning about boundaries, it felt scary - like I was going to offend someone or hurt their feelings if I didn't agree with them all the time. But saying no and setting limits has been one of the most liberating things I've ever done as an artist and entrepreneur.
