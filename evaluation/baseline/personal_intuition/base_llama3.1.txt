What the hell have I been doing, fighting my gut all these years? 

I used to think that trusting my intuition was some woo-woo crap – like, "follow your heart" and all that jazz. But the truth is, it's not about being all touchy-feely; it's about listening to the whispers of your inner compass.

For me, writing has always been a bit of a rollercoaster. I'd get on this high-speed train of enthusiasm, only to crash into self-doubt and overthinking. And then – more often than not – I'd end up stuck in neutral, questioning every word, every sentence, every idea.

But the more I wrote, the more I realized that my intuition was screaming at me to take a different route. To trust that first draft, imperfect as it may be. To let go of perfectionism and just... write.

It's not about ignoring your doubts or rationalizing them away; it's about acknowledging them, but not letting them drive the bus. I've come to understand that my intuition is like a gentle breeze on a summer day – it's there, steady and consistent, guiding me when I need it most.

So, how do you learn to trust your intuition as a writer? It starts with showing up, imperfectly, again and again. With being willing to take risks and make mistakes. And with listening – really listening – to that quiet voice inside.
